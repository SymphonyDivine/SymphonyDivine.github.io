Brown Sugar Cinnamon Pop-Tart Copycat

Prep Time: 1 Hour 30 Minutes     Cook Time: 20 Minutes     Total Time: 2 Hours     Yields: 8 Pop Tarts

Ingredients:


Pie Crust

  • 2 1/2 cups all-purpose flour (plus extra for rolling)
  • 1 cup cold unsalted butter, cubed (2 sticks)
  • 3 tablespoons white sugar
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/3 cup ice cold water

Brown Sugar Filling

  • 2/3 cup brown sugar
  • 1 1/2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons melted butter
  • 2 teaspoons cinnamon
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • pinch of salt

Egg Wash

  • 1 large egg
  • 1 tablespoon heavy cream

Icing

  • 2/3 cup powdered sugar
  • 3 tablespoons maple syrup
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter, browned
  • 1 tablespoon heavy cream
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 3/4 teaspoon cinnamon

Instructions:

    Making The Dough

  1. In a food processor, pulse flour, sugar, and salt to combine.

  2. Add cold cubed butter; pulse until the mixture is crumbly.

  3. Drizzle in the ice water and pulse just until the dough looks pebbly but holds when pressed.

  4. Divide dough into 2 equal sections, wrap each tightly in the shape of a disc, and chill for 1 hour.

  5. On a lightly floured surface, roll each section to 1/8-1/4 inch thick (the thinner the better) into around 13 x 11 inch rectangle; trim edges to make it even.

  6. Cut each rectangle into 8 smaller rectangular pieces (about 3 x 4.5 inches) for 16 total.

    7. Preparing The Filling

  7. In a bowl, mix together brown sugar, all-purpose flour, melted butter, cinnamon, vanilla, and a pinch of salt until evenly combined.

    8. Preparing The Icing

  8. Brown butter (optional): Melt butter in a light-colored pan over medium heat, stirring, until it foams and turns golden with a nutty aroma. Remove from heat and cool slightly. (You can also use regular melted butter.)

  9. Whisk powdered sugar, maple syrup, brown butter (or melted butter), cream, vanilla, and cinnamon until smooth. Set aside.

    10. Assembly & Baking

  10. Line a baking sheet with parchment. Arrange 8 dough rectangles spaced apart.

  11. Add about 2 tablespoons of filling to each, spreading flat and leaving clean edges.

  12. Whisk egg and cream for the egg wash. Brush edges of each bottom rectangle.

  13. Top with another rectangle, press to seal, and crimp edges with a fork.

  14. Poke holes across the tops with a fork for steam vents. Brush tops with egg wash.

  15. Chill the assembled Pop-Tarts in the fridge for 20 to 30 minutes on the baking sheet. Meanwhile, preheat oven to 350°F.

  16. Bake 20 minutes until lightly golden.

  17. Transfer pop tarts to a cooling rack and let cool 10 to 15 minutes, then cover tops with icing. Allow to set before serving.



Pumpkin Churros

Prep Time: 30 Minutes     Cook Time: 30 Minutes     Total Time: 1 Hours     Yields: 10 to 12 Churros

Ingredients:


Cinnamon Sugar Mixture

  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1 teaspoon pumpkin pie spice

Churro Batter

  • 3/4 cup water
  • 2 tablespoons brown sugar
  • 1/2 tablespoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup pumpkin puree
  • 6 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 1/3 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 large eggs
  • vegetable oil, for frying

Optional Dipping Sauces

  • caramel sauce
  • chocolate sauce
  • sweetened condensed milk

Instructions:

    Making The Dough

  1. In a small bowl, whisk together sugar and cinnamon to make the cinnamon-sugar mixture. Set aside.

  2. In a pot, add water, sugar, vanilla extract, pumpkin puree, unsalted butter, and salt.

  3. Set over medium-high heat and whisk together.

  4. Once it comes to a boil, drop the heat to low and add the flour. Use a rubber spatula to mix together until there are no more clumps of flour left, about 3 minutes.

  5. Take off the heat and allow to cool for 6-8 minutes.

  6. Add one egg and mix using a rubber spatula or in a stand mixer until fully incorporated. Add the second egg and mix again until fully incorporated. The dough should be firm, elastic and just a bit sticky.

  7. Transfer into a piping bag fitted with a tip.

  8. Cut parchment paper into small squares. Spray with oil and pipe pumpkins out onto each square. You may need to use scissors to cut the stem off at the end.

  9. Heat oil to 325°F and drop one heart in at a time, making sure to move slowly so it drops into the oil smoothly. Allow it to get golden brown on one side before flipping over. Once it's golden on both sides, transfer to a paper towel to drain excess oil. Repeat until all the churros are fried.

  10. Coat the churros in the cinnamon sugar mixture.

  11. Enjoy with some caramel or chocolate sauce!


Tiramisu Cups

Prep Time: 1 Hour     Total Time: 1 Hours     Yields: 12 Cups

Ingredients:


Cream Filling

  • 5 egg yolks
  • 2/3 cup white sugar
  • 1 1/3 cup heavy cream
  • 16 ounces mascarpone cheese
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract

For Assembly

  • 2 packages ladyfingers, 7 ounces each
  • 1 1/2 cup cold espresso
  • 3 tablespoons brown sugar

Instructions:

    Prepare The Cream Filling

  1. In a medium-sized mixing bowl, whisk together the egg yolks and sugar until thickened and lighter in color.

  2. In a separate large mixing bowl, beat the heavy cream and vanilla extract until stiff peaks form.

  3. Gently fold the mascarpone cheese into the egg yolk mixture until smooth and creamy.

  4. Gradually fold in the whipped cream until the mixture is well combined and creamy. Be gentle to maintain the fluffy texture.

    5. Preparing The Filling

  5. In a shallow dish, mix the cold espresso with the brown sugar until the sugar is dissolved.
  6. Dip each ladyfinger into the espresso mixture for a few seconds, ensuring they are soaked but not soggy.

    7. Assemble The Tiramisu Cups

  7. In individual serving cups or glasses, place a layer of soaked ladyfingers at the bottom.

  8. Spoon a layer of the cream filling over the ladyfingers, spreading it evenly.

  9. Repeat the layers, alternating between soaked ladyfingers and cream filling until the cups are filled, ending with a layer of cream on top.

    10. Chill And Serve

  10. Cover the tiramisu cups with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 4 hours, or overnight, to allow the flavors to meld and the dessert to set.

  11. Before serving, you can dust the tops with cocoa powder or grated chocolate for decoration if desired.

  12. Serve chilled and enjoy your delicious individual tiramisu cups!
